Overall Summary

Performance: Xpro India reported robust growth in FY25 despite global headwinds. FY25 Net Sales reached an all-time high of ₹535.28 Cr (+15% YoY). The Coex division was a primary driver with 32% growth in Q4.

Margins: EBITDA margins were impacted by a strategic shift in product mix (higher contribution from lower-margin Coex business) and conscious pricing adjustments in the dielectric segment to retain market share ahead of capacity expansion. Management believes margins have bottomed out.

Expansion: The Barjora expansion is imminent (dry runs ongoing), and the UAE subsidiary is progressing well (dry runs expected Q4 2024). These expansions position Xpro to capture global "China Plus One" demand.

Key Takeaways

Capacity Expansion Status

Barjora: Dry runs underway; commercial production expected ~Sept 2025. Revenue potential ₹150-200 Cr/year.
UAE (RAK): Construction ongoing; dry runs expected Q4 2025. Aimed at global markets (US/Europe).

Segment Dynamics

Dielectric Films (Biax): ~30% domestic market share. Running near 100% utilization. High entry barriers. Margins 30-50%.
Coex Sheets: Strong 25% volume growth driven by refrigerator liners. Lower margin (5-7%), but drives top-line.

Strategic Pricing

Management reduced dielectric pricing by ~5-7% to retain customers before new capacity comes online. This was a strategic choice, not forced by commodity prices. Prices are now stabilizing or reversing.
