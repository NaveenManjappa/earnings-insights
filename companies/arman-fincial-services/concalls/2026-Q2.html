Executive Summary

Q2 FY26 marks a pivotal turnaround for Arman Financial Services. After facing industry-wide headwinds, the company reported a profit of ₹8 crores, reversing the loss from the previous quarter. Management highlighted signs of stabilization in the microfinance portfolio, with impairment costs trending down significantly and collection efficiencies improving to 95.6%. While disbursements in MFI remain calibrated, the Non-MFI segment (MSME & LAP) continues to drive growth, expanding 29% YoY.

Key Takeaways

STRATEGY

Maintained a calibrated approach in Microfinance (AUM ₹1,507 Cr) while expanding MSME/LAP (AUM ₹623 Cr). Focus remains on portfolio quality over headline growth.

OPERATIONS

Structural reforms are yielding results. Separation of credit underwriting from recovery is now live in 50% of branches (196 branches), leading to better accountability.

OUTLOOK

Optimistic for H2 FY26. October/November trends (festive season) show strong demand with MFI disbursements reaching ₹140 Cr/month. Expect continued stabilization.
